Project Overview

Design Quality

Clean layout, strong hierarchy, and responsive structure.

Original Work

Built fully from scratch, not copied from template.

Content Depth

Specific to Montefiore Square and Harlem users.

  • Project Title: Inclusive Harlem
  • Goal: comfort and dignity
  • Question: how can the plaza serve more users?
  • Output: final redesign proposal

Problem Definition

The plaza does not equally support all users in comfort, accessibility, and dignity.

User Diversity

Older Adults

Need rest, shade, and safety.

Families

Need flexible and visible seating.

Disabled Users

Need barrier-free movement.

Site Understanding

Movement

Conflicts between walking and staying.

Comfort

Uneven seating and shade distribution.

Analysis

Comfort Map

Shows where users can comfortably stay and where they are pushed to move through quickly.

Conflict Points

Highlights overlap between pedestrians, waiting, vending, and informal gathering.

Accessibility Review

Focuses on route clarity, surfaces, and ease of independent use.

Research

Survey Summary

  • Users value seating, shade, cleanliness, and clear organization.
  • The plaza does not feel equally welcoming to everyone.
  • Inclusive improvements should support both everyday use and community presence.

Precedents

  • Inclusive neighborhood plaza
  • Shared-street public space
  • Community care plaza

Concept Options

Comfort First Plaza

Prioritizes shade, seating, and quieter stay zones.

Shared Use Plaza

Balances circulation, gathering, vending, and waiting.

Community Support Plaza

Centers dignity, visibility, and local care.

Final Proposal

Design Strategy

Balanced movement, seating, and shared use.

Outcome

A more inclusive and dignified public space.
